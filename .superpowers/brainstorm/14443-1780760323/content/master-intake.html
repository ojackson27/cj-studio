Section 1: Master Intake & Classification

The 5 questions that determine which track a client lands on — and how the answers map to site types

The 5 Classification Questions

These go into a Tally.so form sent to every new client before the first call.

Q1 — Business Type
What does your business do?
Food & drink / Trades & construction / Professional service / Creative & arts / Other
Q2 — Primary Goal
What do you most want the site to do?
Get people to visit / Get quote enquiries / Build credibility & trust / Showcase my work
Q3 — Visual vs Information
How important is atmosphere & visual feel vs detailed information?
Mostly feel (1) ←——→ Mostly information (5)
Q4 — Photography
Do you have professional photos of your business/work?
Yes, lots / Yes, a few / No — need sourcing / No — AI generation fine
Q5 — Competitors
Share 2–3 websites you admire (in or out of your industry)
Free text — reveals aesthetic preferences better than any other question

How Answers Map to Tracks

Q1 + Q2 do most of the work. Q3 breaks ties. Q4–5 feed the design direction.

🍽 Hospitality
Q1: Food & drink · Q2: Get people to visit · Q3: 1–2 (mostly feel)
Tools: Lenis scroll, Framer Motion reveals, Unsplash/client food photography, Google Maps
🏗 Trades & Construction
Q1: Trades · Q2: Get quote enquiries · Q3: 3–4 (balanced)
Tools: Project gallery, Google Reviews widget, Formspree quote form, trust badges
💼 Professional Services
Q1: Professional service · Q2: Build credibility · Q3: 4–5 (info-heavy)
Tools: Cal.com scheduling, Sanity CMS, Resend forms, clean typography-led layout
🎨 Creative & Portfolio
Q1: Creative & arts · Q2: Showcase work · Q3: 1 (pure feel)
Tools: GSAP ScrollTrigger, React Three Fiber, custom cursors, full-bleed visual layout
Edge case — "Other" or conflicting answers
Human review required. You and Josh decide the track on the discovery call, then fill in the brief manually.
